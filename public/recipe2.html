STEP 1 @ Place chicken pieces in a large stock pot. @ When you've cut as much chicken meat as you'd like, simply add it to a large pot and you're done. @ If you'd prefer, you can also remove the skin, though this isn't essential. STEP 2 @ Add water to cover, salt, and bay leaf. @ Add enough water to cover all of your chicken, but don't feel the need to over-do it . @ If your soup is too thick, you can always add more water later, while waiting for a watery soup to reduce is time-consuming. @ For added flavor, you may optionally add the chicken bouillon at this point as well. STEP 3 @ Cover the pot and bring to boil. @ Over medium-high heat, this should take about 8-10 minutes, but may take more or less time depending on how much water you use. STEP 4 @ Uncover and skim off the top froth. @ When your water starts boiling, remove the lid and remove the froth at the top with a wooden spoon. @ This prevents the water from boiling over. STEP 5 @ Allow to simmer about 2 1/4 hours. @ Your goal is to make the chicken meat so soft that it falls off the bone. @ As your soup simmers, periodically check on it to ensure that the chicken is cooking nicely and to skim any froth off the top of the pot. STEP 6 @ Add the onions, celery, and carrots and a pinch or two of parsley. @ After your chicken has cooked for a few hours, you may add your vegetables, which take less time to cook. STEP 7 @ Allow to simmer about 45 minutes. Stir periodically to ensure even mixing and to eliminate top froth.
